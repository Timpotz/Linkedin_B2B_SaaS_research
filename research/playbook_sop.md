# Playbook: LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy for B2B SaaS

## Purpose

This playbook is for B2B SaaS founders, marketers, content leads, and operator-creators who want LinkedIn to become a serious trust-building and demand-shaping channel, not a place to post disconnected updates.

The research base behind this playbook includes 10 practitioners whose work touches B2B SaaS content, founder-led marketing, LinkedIn distribution, zero-click marketing, creator-led growth, product-led growth, and revenue-oriented content strategy. The aim here is not to give every expert equal space. It is to synthesize the strongest and most useful patterns into an operating system a real B2B SaaS team could run.

## Experts Researched

The 10 experts researched were Devin Reed, Dave Gerhardt, Amanda Natividad, Tas Bober, Alex Boyd, Richard van der Blom, Udi Ledergor, Brendan Hufford, Lashay Lewis, and Tom Hunt (source: [repository sources.md](https://github.com/Timpotz/Linkedin_B2B_SaaS_research/blob/main/research/sources.md)).

## Core Argument

The best LinkedIn organic strategy for B2B SaaS is not "post more." It is to build a visible point of view for a specific buyer, support that point of view with proof, and use comments, posts, DMs, and repeatable content formats as one operating system.

I would summarize the strategy this way:

1. Build for a specific audience, not a broad market. A relevant network is part of the strategy, not a secondary distribution detail (source: Richard van der Blom, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_if-i-were-building-a-b2b-linkedin-strategy-activity-7373604519294771200-scLG); Richard van der Blom, [article/video summary from 01.08.2025](https://coronatodays.com/how-to-get-more-visibility-engagement-on-linkedin-in-2025-with-richard-van-der-blom/)).
2. Create content that delivers value inside LinkedIn before asking for a click. Buyers often form opinions from what they see in-platform long before they visit a company website (source: Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-youre-still-trying-to-drive-traffic-activity-7341483658664386564-1FNm); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).
3. Use comments as research, distribution, and relationship-building, not as cleanup after publishing (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Tas Bober, [episode from 29.01.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/What-Elite-B2B-Marketers-Do-Differently-to-Win-%28with-Tas-Bober%29-id6083309-id774866908)).
4. Convert winning ideas into a repeatable series or media engine only after they show resonance. Expansion should follow evidence, not ambition (source: Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156); Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post from Aug 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_b2b-podcast-activity-7097108816034426880-IZ1K)).

## Playbook SOP

### Step 1: Define the Buyer Before Defining the Content

Start with one buyer group, one painful problem, and one defensible angle. I would not begin with generic "content pillars" because they often produce broad, interchangeable posts. A better starting point is: who needs to believe something different, what problem do they already feel, and what can this company credibly say that others are not saying clearly enough? (source: Richard van der Blom, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_if-i-were-building-a-b2b-linkedin-strategy-activity-7373604519294771200-scLG); Tas Bober, [episode from 29.01.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/What-Elite-B2B-Marketers-Do-Differently-to-Win-%28with-Tas-Bober%29-id6083309-id774866908)).

### Step 2: Build a Proof Bank

Before building a posting calendar, collect evidence. The proof bank should include sales-call objections, customer-success conversations, product usage observations, customer examples, founder notes, and internal experiments. This prevents the content from becoming generic advice dressed up as thought leadership (source: Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).

For each proof-bank entry, record the buyer language, the source of the insight, the funnel stage, and the possible LinkedIn angle. This makes the content operation easier to audit and prevents the team from mistaking opinions for evidence (source: Lashay Lewis, [LinkedIn BOFU template post from Sep 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lashaylewis_contentstrategy-saascontentstrategy-contentmarketing-activity-7114594978147786754-INT9); Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156)).

### Step 3: Align the Profile and Network With the Strategy

The profile should make the strategic position obvious: who the person helps, what problem they understand, and why the viewer should trust them. The network should then be shaped around the same audience: customers, prospects, partners, peers, category creators, analysts, and respected operators in the buyer's world (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Richard van der Blom, [article/video summary from 01.08.2025](https://coronatodays.com/how-to-get-more-visibility-engagement-on-linkedin-in-2025-with-richard-van-der-blom/)).

### Step 4: Write for In-Feed Consumption

A LinkedIn post should not require a click before it becomes useful. The post itself should contain the lesson, proof, example, or argument. Links can still be used, especially for launches or high-intent moments, but the core value should be delivered before the click (source: Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-youre-still-trying-to-drive-traffic-activity-7341483658664386564-1FNm); Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31)).

### Step 5: Use Three Content Lanes

I would run the content system through three recurring lanes:

1. Buyer insight posts: posts based on customer pain, objections, decision criteria, and language from the market (source: Lashay Lewis, [LinkedIn BOFU template post from Sep 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lashaylewis_contentstrategy-saascontentstrategy-contentmarketing-activity-7114594978147786754-INT9); Brendan Hufford, [episode from 12.02.2025](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-content-strategy-mistake-costing-you-growth/id1736811325?i=1000691346857)).
2. Proof posts: posts based on original research, operating data, customer stories, experiments, or visible examples (source: Devin Reed, [LinkedIn video research post from 22.01.2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_if-youre-posting-videos-on-linkedin-youll-activity-7287871063961550848-ynpM); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).
3. Point-of-view posts: posts that make a real argument about the category, the buyer problem, or a common mistake in the market (source: Dave Gerhardt, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_there-are-more-b2b-marketers-outside-of-saas-activity-7307019426585804800-MB5W); Udi Ledergor, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://pod.co/growthcolony/udi-ledergor)).

This mix matters because a feed made only of opinions becomes thin, while a feed made only of proof can feel inert. Strong LinkedIn content needs both evidence and interpretation.

### Step 6: Treat Comments as a Strategic Surface

Comments should be used before and after posting. Before posting, they help test language, identify objections, and find active conversations in the right network. After posting, they help extend the useful life of an idea and create relationship surface area with the right people (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Richard van der Blom, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_if-i-were-building-a-b2b-linkedin-strategy-activity-7373604519294771200-scLG)).

### Step 7: Publish Useful Thinking Before It Is Over-Processed

There is a useful distinction between unpolished and careless. I would encourage publishing ideas while they are still close to the work, as long as they are specific, useful, and true. Over-produced thought leadership often loses the texture that made the idea worth sharing in the first place (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Brendan Hufford, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_i-waited-2-years-after-starting-growth-sprints-activity-7391466823344422912-jU5q)).

### Step 8: Build One Recognizable Series

The fastest way to make the strategy feel coherent is to create one repeatable series. It could be a weekly teardown, a field-notes series, original research, customer-objection posts, or a public experiment. A series gives the audience a reason to remember the work and gives the team a structure for repurposing it later (source: Devin Reed, [LinkedIn video research post from 22.01.2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_if-youre-posting-videos-on-linkedin-youll-activity-7287871063961550848-ynpM); Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-you-want-to-try-zero-click-marketing-for-activity-7442261462473150464-QfTK); Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156)).

### Step 9: Connect Feed-Level Trust to Revenue Assets

LinkedIn should not be isolated from the revenue system. Strong posts should eventually inform BOFU templates, comparison assets, landing-page copy, founder narratives, sales talk tracks, and customer proof assets (source: Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Tas Bober, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tasbober_87-of-b2b-buyers-trust-content-creators-activity-7351219085059248129-n9nR); Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31)).

### Step 10: Expand Only What Shows Resonance

Once a theme starts working, expand it into a video, carousel, podcast clip, newsletter section, sales asset, or founder post. The important constraint is sequencing: prove the idea in a smaller format before turning it into a larger content engine (source: Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156); Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post from Aug 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_b2b-podcast-activity-7097108816034426880-IZ1K)).

## Weekly Operating Rhythm

1. Monday: refresh the proof bank by interviewing one salesperson, one customer-facing teammate, or one customer. This keeps content anchored in buyer reality (source: Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Brendan Hufford, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_i-waited-2-years-after-starting-growth-sprints-activity-7391466823344422912-jU5q)).
2. Tuesday: draft one post in each lane: buyer insight, proof, and point of view. This keeps the content balanced (source: Lashay Lewis, [LinkedIn BOFU template post from Sep 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lashaylewis_contentstrategy-saascontentstrategy-contentmarketing-activity-7114594978147786754-INT9); Devin Reed, [LinkedIn video research post from 22.01.2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_if-youre-posting-videos-on-linkedin-youll-activity-7287871063961550848-ynpM); Dave Gerhardt, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_there-are-more-b2b-marketers-outside-of-saas-activity-7307019426585804800-MB5W)).
3. Wednesday: publish the strongest post in full on LinkedIn without relying on a click to make it valuable (source: Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-youre-still-trying-to-drive-traffic-activity-7341483658664386564-1FNm); Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31)).
4. Thursday: spend the main content block on thoughtful comments across ICP-relevant posts, then record which comments created replies, profile views, or DMs (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Tas Bober, [episode from 29.01.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/What-Elite-B2B-Marketers-Do-Differently-to-Win-%28with-Tas-Bober%29-id6083309-id774866908)).
5. Friday: choose the week's strongest signal and decide whether it should become another post, a second format, a sales asset, or part of the flagship series (source: Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156); Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post from Aug 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_b2b-podcast-activity-7097108816034426880-IZ1K)).

## Metrics

The operating metrics should reflect buyer movement, not just engagement volume:

- ICP comments, not total comments, because relevance is more important than applause from the wrong audience (source: Richard van der Blom, [article/video summary from 01.08.2025](https://coronatodays.com/how-to-get-more-visibility-engagement-on-linkedin-in-2025-with-richard-van-der-blom/)).
- Profile views after posts and comments, because they indicate that the content or comment created enough interest for someone to investigate the person behind it (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd)).
- Qualified DMs and sales conversations started by content, because buyer movement matters more than reactions (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046)).
- Reusable buyer language from comments, DMs, sales replies, and customer conversations, because the audience's words should improve future content and sales assets (source: Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).
- Ideas that can become landing pages, sales assets, newsletters, founder posts, webinars, or research reports, because good LinkedIn content should feed the broader growth system (source: Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156); Tom Hunt, [episode from 18.09.2025](https://www.tomhunt.io/blog/th-saas-that-app)).

## Where Experts Disagree

### 1. Zero-Click Value vs Link-Driven Traffic

What Amanda Natividad Recommends: Amanda Natividad recommends designing content for in-platform consumption and argues against depending on clicks for value delivery (source: Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-youre-still-trying-to-drive-traffic-activity-7341483658664386564-1FNm); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).

What Dave Gerhardt Recommends: Dave Gerhardt argues that LinkedIn can also be a direct launch channel when a founder or company already has attention from the right audience (source: Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31)).

Which side I take and why: I take Amanda's side for day-to-day publishing. Most posts should carry their value inside LinkedIn. Dave's link-driven approach is strongest for launches, events, waitlists, and high-intent announcements where the audience is already warmed up.

### 2. Comment-First Growth vs Flagship Content-First Growth

What Alex Boyd Recommends: Alex Boyd gives comments unusually high strategic weight, treating them as a major lever for visibility, profile views, DMs, and business outcomes (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd)).

What  Devin Reed Recommends: Devin Reed's work points more toward a repeatable content engine where strong ideas expand into multiple formats over time (source: Devin Reed, [episode from 26.02.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/How-to-Build-a-Multi-Million-Dollar-Content-Engine-%28with-Nick-Cegelski%29-id6083309-id783063156)).

Which side I take and why: I take Devin's side as the strategic center. Comments are a powerful accelerant, but a flagship series gives the team a durable asset. Without a durable body of work, the comment strategy can become active but hard to compound.

### 3. Half-Baked Ideas vs Structured Bodies of Work

What Alex Boyd Recommends: Alex Boyd recommends sharing work-in-progress thinking because unfinished ideas can create useful conversation and reduce production friction (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd)).

What Amanda Natividad Recommends: Amanda Natividad leans toward structured bodies of work, retrieval, proof, and organized publishing (source: Amanda Natividad, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_if-you-want-to-try-zero-click-marketing-for-activity-7442261462473150464-QfTK); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).

Which side I take and why: for a company account or founder-led company system, I take Amanda's side. Structure makes the system easier to sustain, measure, and repurpose. I would still borrow Alex's speed and tolerance for imperfect ideas, but I would place them inside a recognizable format.

### 4. Platform Mechanics vs Message Substance

What Richard van der Blom Recommends: Richard van der Blom emphasizes LinkedIn mechanics: relevance, recency, network quality, profile optimization, DM conversion, and post-publication behavior (source: Richard van der Blom, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_if-i-were-building-a-b2b-linkedin-strategy-activity-7373604519294771200-scLG); Richard van der Blom, [article/video summary from 01.08.2025](https://coronatodays.com/how-to-get-more-visibility-engagement-on-linkedin-in-2025-with-richard-van-der-blom/)).

What Brendan Hufford Recommends: Brendan Hufford warns against content that is technically optimized but strategically hollow, arguing for original, human-relevant content instead (source: Brendan Hufford, [episode from 12.02.2025](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-content-strategy-mistake-costing-you-growth/id1736811325?i=1000691346857); Brendan Hufford, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_i-waited-2-years-after-starting-growth-sprints-activity-7391466823344422912-jU5q)).

Which side I take and why: I take Brendan's side first. Message substance should precede optimization. Once the content has buyer relevance and proof, Richard's mechanics become useful leverage.

### 5. Bold Differentiation vs Buyer Confidence

What Udi Ledergor Recommends: Udi Ledergor argues that being different can be more useful than trying to be a marginally better version of competitors in a crowded B2B market (source: Udi Ledergor, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://pod.co/growthcolony/udi-ledergor)).

What Tas Bober and Lashay Lewis Recommends: Tas Bober and Lashay Lewis put more emphasis on buyer confidence, revenue relevance, decision support, and practical proof (source: Tas Bober, [episode from 29.01.2025](https://castbox.fm/episode/What-Elite-B2B-Marketers-Do-Differently-to-Win-%28with-Tas-Bober%29-id6083309-id774866908); Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046)).

Which side I take and why: I take Tas and Lashay's side for this SOP. Differentiation matters, but proof and buyer confidence should come first. Boldness without substance is just a louder weak claim.


# What I Rejected and Why

1. I rejected paid amplification as a core step. Tas Bober makes a credible case that creator credibility can become more powerful when paired with thought-leader ads, but this is an organic content playbook. Paid distribution should be added only after the team has proven that the message, audience, and content format work organically (source: Tas Bober, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tasbober_87-of-b2b-buyers-trust-content-creators-activity-7351219085059248129-n9nR)).

2. I rejected AI landing-page scale as a main component. Tom Hunt's AI-powered landing-page testing work is interesting, but it belongs to a broader SEO and experimentation system, not the core LinkedIn organic loop (source: Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2026](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_300k-words-1600-landing-pages-25k-impressions-activity-7414957557250764800-HYw9)).

3. I rejected virality as the north-star metric. Tom Hunt's LinkedIn notes point to comments, consistency, and tension-based hooks as drivers of viral posts, but B2B SaaS LinkedIn should optimize for qualified buyer movement rather than broad attention by itself (source: Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2024](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_10m-impressions-70k-likes-5k-comments-activity-7249680147614765056-LtNL); Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd)).

4. I rejected bold branding as a default tactic. Udi Ledergor's argument for courageous differentiation is valuable, but many teams copy the surface of boldness before they have a strong enough point of view. In this playbook, differentiation should come from sharper insight first and expression second (source: Udi Ledergor, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://pod.co/growthcolony/udi-ledergor)).

## My Original Idea

### The Audience Signal Ledger

My original idea is to maintain an Audience Signal Ledger that combines three things: a proof bank, a comment-to-post backlog, and a buyer-language log.

I did not find this exact operating system in the collected sources. It is my synthesis of Alex Boyd's comment-first logic, Lashay Lewis's revenue-oriented content planning, and Amanda Natividad's emphasis on proof and retrievability (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd); Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Amanda Natividad, [episode from 14.04.2026](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BVRE-0fHlV4)).

The ledger would track comments that generated replies, profile views, DMs, or sales mentions. It would also capture exact buyer language from comments, DMs, sales calls, and customer success notes. Each entry would be tagged by buyer stage and content lane, then reviewed weekly to decide whether it should become a full post, a sales asset, a landing-page section, or part of the flagship series.

Why it could work: comments are a low-cost way to test language against a real audience before investing in a full post, and buyer language is often the missing bridge between social content and revenue assets.


## Weaknesses of This Playbook

1. This playbook assumes the company has some operator credibility or access to customer truth. It is weaker for teams that have no proof, no customer proximity, and no clear market point of view (source: Lashay Lewis, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/the-dave-gerhardt-show-from-exit-five-2628644/episodes/how-to-drive-revenue-through-c-281221046); Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31)).

2. The research set leans toward English-speaking B2B SaaS operators with visible personal brands. That may overstate how easily founder-led or creator-led content transfers to less personality-driven companies (source: [repository README](https://github.com/Timpotz/Linkedin_B2B_SaaS_research/blob/main/readme.md); [repository sources.md](https://github.com/Timpotz/Linkedin_B2B_SaaS_research/blob/main/research/sources.md)).

3. Some evidence comes from YouTube automatic captions, public episode notes, and partial transcripts rather than perfect full transcripts. The evidence is good enough for a practical playbook, but not as strong as a formal interview corpus (source: [repository README](https://github.com/Timpotz/Linkedin_B2B_SaaS_research/blob/main/readme.md)).

4. The playbook assumes LinkedIn is a meaningful attention channel for the target market. That will not be true in every B2B SaaS niche (source: Dave Gerhardt, [episode from 07.11.2024](https://thetransactionpod.com/episodes/ai-idea-starters-influencers-linkedin-with-dave-gerhardt-the-transaction-ep-31); Richard van der Blom, [LinkedIn post noted as approx. 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_if-i-were-building-a-b2b-linkedin-strategy-activity-7373604519294771200-scLG)).

5. The playbook may show trust signals before pipeline. Profile views, relevant comments, DMs, and sales mentions can appear before closed revenue, so the system needs patience and careful interpretation (source: Alex Boyd, [episode from 07.04.2025](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2434811/episodes/16916670-the-unlimited-potential-of-linkedin-commenting-with-alex-boyd)).

6. LinkedIn mechanics can change. Recommendations about relevance, recency, network quality, and post-publication behavior are useful, but they should not be treated as permanent laws of distribution (source: Richard van der Blom, [article/video summary from 01.08.2025](https://coronatodays.com/how-to-get-more-visibility-engagement-on-linkedin-in-2025-with-richard-van-der-blom/)).

7. Content cannot compensate for weak product-market fit. Tom Hunt's broader point about product-led growth and retention is a useful warning: content compounds best when the product and customer outcomes can support the story (source: Tom Hunt, [episode from 18.09.2025](https://www.tomhunt.io/blog/th-saas-that-app)).

## Who I Would NOT Recommend Following First and Why
Of the 10 experts researched, I would not recommend Udi Ledergor or Tom Hunt as primary guides for this exact LinkedIn organic SOP.

I would not recommend Udi Ledergor because his strongest captured material in this repo is about courageous B2B brand differentiation, not day-to-day LinkedIn organic execution. His thinking is valuable once a team already has a proof base and a clear point of view. As a starting point, however, it may encourage teams to copy the surface expression of boldness before they have earned it with substance (source: Udi Ledergor, [episode from 29.01.2026](https://pod.co/growthcolony/udi-ledergor)).

I would not recommend Tom Hunt because his strongest ideas in this set naturally pull toward a broader media-and-growth system that includes podcasting, product-led growth, retention, and AI landing-page experimentation. That is valuable later, but it is not the cleanest starting point for someone trying to learn LinkedIn organic content strategy specifically (source: Tom Hunt, [episode from 18.09.2025](https://www.tomhunt.io/blog/th-saas-that-app); Tom Hunt, [LinkedIn post from Aug 2023](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomhuntio_b2b-podcast-activity-7097108816034426880-IZ1K)).


This is not a dismissal of either expert. It is a sequencing judgment. I would read Udi later for brand courage and Tom later for broader content-led growth. For the first phase of a LinkedIn organic system, I would prioritize Alex Boyd, Amanda Natividad, Lashay Lewis, Tas Bober, Dave Gerhardt, Devin Reed, Brendan Hufford, and Richard van der Blom.
